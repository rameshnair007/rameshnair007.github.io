👋 Hey there, I'm Ramesh

I'm a Senior Applied Machine Learning Engineer with 7+ years of experience building computer vision systems that make sense of the world — from deep forests to dense urban grids — using satellite imagery and geospatial data. My work lives at the intersection of AI, environmental monitoring, and scalable system design.

I thrive on turning research ideas into real-world products, and I believe in using machine learning not just for automation, but for planetary impact.

🚀 What I'm Working On

At Planet Labs, I lead the development of robust ML pipelines that power large-scale satellite imagery analytics. Here’s what I’m currently focused on:

  • Building high-performance detection and segmentation models for infrastructure and land use
  • Designing weakly supervised learning workflows for rapid model iteration
  • Developing multi-modal fusion strategies across temporal, spectral, and SAR data
  • Scaling ML pipelines for global coverage with minimal latency

I’m also deeply involved in improving model interpretability and collaborating across teams to deploy solutions that are used by scientists, governments, and humanitarian organizations.

📚 A Bit of Background

I hold a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering from Rochester Institute of Technology, where I focused on computer vision and pattern recognition. Prior to Planet, I was at Siemens Corporate Research, working on novel approaches in anomaly detection and segmentation.

My work has been published at top-tier conferences like ICML, and I also hold a patent in weakly supervised learning.
